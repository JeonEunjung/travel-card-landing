## Design Context

### Users
Japanese tourists visiting Korea, specifically medical tourism patients (dermatology, plastic surgery) and general travelers. They're browsing on mobile, likely on their phone while researching their upcoming Korea trip or currently in Korea. They need to trust this enough to consider putting money into it. The primary emotion: "this is legit, and it saves me money."

### Brand Personality
Trustworthy, direct, warm. Not a bank (too cold), not a travel app (too playful). A confident financial tool that respects your intelligence and your money. Think: the calm confidence of a Suica card meets the clarity of a good currency converter.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Clean, confident, warm. Not sterile-fintech, not bubbly-travel.
- **Theme**: Light mode primary (Japanese tourists browsing during daytime, travel research context). Dark mode NOT needed for this landing page.
- **Colors**: TBD brand (no purple gradients, no generic fintech blue). Warm neutrals as base. A single strong accent for CTAs. Consider teal/green for trust + warm orange for action.
- **Anti-references**: Generic fintech landing pages with purple gradients. WOWPASS's orange-coral hero. Anything that looks like "every other prepaid card."
- **References**: Wise's clarity and whitespace. Suica's functional confidence. Stripe's information density done elegantly.

### Design Principles
1. **Numbers do the selling.** "7,700円消える" is more persuasive than any adjective. Show concrete savings, not abstract benefits.
2. **Japanese-first, not translated.** The page should feel native to a Japanese reader, not like a Korean site machine-translated. Natural Japanese UX patterns, reading flow, and politeness levels.
3. **Mobile-first, mobile-only mindset.** 80%+ traffic will be mobile. Every element must work at 375px first.
4. **Trust through restraint.** Financial services earn trust by NOT trying too hard. Clean typography, clear numbers, minimal decoration. The less flashy it is, the more trustworthy it feels.
5. **Measure, don't market.** This is a hypothesis validation tool. Every section exists to produce a data point. Beautiful is good, but measurable is mandatory.

### Technical Constraints
- Static HTML, deployable to Vercel/Netlify
- Multi-language: Japanese (primary), Korean, English
- GA4 event tracking (amount_selected, preregister_click, preregister_complete, survey_click)
- Mobile-first responsive
- LCP < 2 seconds
- No framework dependencies
